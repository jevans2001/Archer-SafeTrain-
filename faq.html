Electrical Safety Training FAQ

Prerequisites and Level Requirements
Can I skip Level 1 if I need Level 2 training?

Yes. Level 1 is not a prerequisite for Level 2. If your role requires Level 2, you can attend Level 2 training directly without completing Level 1 first.

Can I skip Level 2 if I need Level 3 training?

No. Level 2 is a prerequisite for Level 3. You must complete Level 2 before you can attend Level 3 training.

Format and Attendance
Can these trainings be attended remotely?

Only Level 1 training can be attended remotely. Level 2 and Level 3 training must be completed in-person at HQ.

Level 2 and Level 3 are in-person only. Work with your manager to schedule dedicated onsite time to attend these sessions.
I don't have time to attend the training. What should I do?

This training is mandatory to perform this type of dangerous work. If you do not attend the training you are not allowed to complete the work.

Disciplinary action will be taken if you are discovered performing work that you have not been trained to do.
Validity and Retraining
How long is the training valid? How often is retraining required?

All training levels are valid for 2 years. New employees, or employees who are performing new electrical tasks, may not perform any of those tasks until properly trained.

Existing employees who completed previous Level 2 training must be retrained by the end of 2025, but may continue performing work during this transition period.
Authorization and Quiz Requirements
Am I authorized to perform work immediately after training?

This depends on the level:

Level 1 and Level 2: You are authorized to perform work immediately after completing training.

Level 3: A skills evaluation and manager approval are required before you receive authorization. You are not automatically authorized after attending the training session.

The quiz and skills evaluation can be failed, and will result in authorization being withheld.
Will there be a test or other type of authorization?

Yes. Level 2 and Level 3 require attendees to successfully pass a quiz or skills evaluation. Failure results in authorization being withheld until the requirements are met.

Compliance and Enforcement
How do I know if members of my team are trained?

Employees performing electrical work are required to wear physical badges while at work. These badges will have a sticker placed on the back that indicates what level of training the employee has completed.

What do I do if I encounter someone performing work who is not authorized?

Report the person to a nearby supervisor immediately. Supervisors must pause the work immediately.

Supervisors must also report this incident as a "near-miss" to Archer EHS using the Incident Report Form.
Resources and Contact
Is there a program that provides general guidance for electrical work?

Yes. Archer's Electrical Safety Program can serve as a reference for best practices and requirements when performing electrical work. Contact Archer EHS for access.
